The Scan on Biblioteca Digital Mexicana

The Mexican Digital Library, BDMx, was set up by four important Mexican cultural institutions. These institutions decided to join efforts to create a Mexican multi-institutional digital library: they would contribute relevant historical and cultural documents from their holdings.

The Scan on World Digital Library

The World Digital Library (WDL) was a project of the U.S. Library of Congress, with the support of UNESCO, and contributions from libraries, archives, museums, educational institutions, and international organizations around the world.

The Digital Scan on Mesolore

Mesolore has been created by Liza Bakewell and Byron Hamann. Mesolore was developed at Brown University in collaboration with Prolarti Enterprises, LLC, and with funding from the National Science Foundation, the National Endowment for the Humanities, the Davis Educational Foundation, and the Ford Foundation.

The Scan on INAh Mediateca

The Instituto Nacional de Antropología e Historia is a Mexican federal government bureau established in 1939 to guarantee the research, preservation, protection, and promotion of the prehistoric, archaeological, anthropological, historical, and paleontological heritage of Mexico.

The Facsimile published by ADEVA

The Akademische Druck- und Verlagsanstalt (ADEVA or Akad. Dr.- u. Verl.-Anst.) Is an Austrian book publisher in Graz, which specializes primarily in the publication of complex facsimile editions.

The PDF File by Aztec Empire Embassy

PDF file produced by Aztec Empire Embassy. This file can be printed as a book.

The Facsimile seld by Facsimile Finder

Facsimile Finder is a supplier of books, specializing in facsimile editions.

The Facsimile seld by Ziereis Facsimiles

Ziereis Facsimiles is a Facsimiles sales organization formed by Georg and Christian Ziereis. They often cooperate with governments, universities and libraries.
